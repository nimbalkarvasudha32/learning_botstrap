Yoga

Yoga is a mind-body practice rooted in ancient Indian philosophy, combining physical postures (asanas), breathing techniques (pranayama), and meditation to promote overall well-being

.

Vision

Vision represents a deeper understanding and purpose behind the practice of yoga. It can refer to a personal goal or intention, such as achieving inner peace, physical well-being, or spiritual growth. In a broader sense, it may also be the name of a yoga studio or wellness center focused on holistic practices and mindful living. Spiritually, Yoga Vision can symbolize inner awareness, clarity, and insight, often linked to the concept of the third eye or focused attention (*drishti*) in yoga philosophy. Overall, it reflects the connection between body, mind, and spirit through intentional practice.

Mission

The mission of yoga is to create harmony between the body, mind, and spirit. Through physical postures (asanas), breath control (pranayama), meditation, and ethical living, yoga aims to promote overall well-being, inner peace, and self-awareness. It encourages a balanced lifestyle, reduces stress, and fosters compassion toward oneself and others. Ultimately, the mission of yoga is to guide individuals toward self-realization and a deeper connection with life..

History

Yoga is an ancient practice that originated in India over 5,000 years ago. Rooted in spiritual traditions, it began as a way to connect the body, mind, and spirit through meditation, breath control, and ethical living. The earliest references to yoga are found in the Vedas, where it was closely tied to ritual and spiritual discipline. Over time, it evolved into a more structured system with the introduction of Patanjali’s Yoga Sutras, which outlined the Eight Limbs of Yoga—a guide to achieving self-realization and inner peace.
